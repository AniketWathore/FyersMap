Details about each feature:

Volume Bubbles:
Bookmap's Volume Bubbles (or Volume Dots) provide a real-time, visual representation of executed trades (aggressive volume) layered over the liquidity heatmap, allowing traders to see who is controlling the market—buyers or sellers—at specific price levels and times. These bubbles act as a footprint, helping to identify momentum, exhaustion, and hidden imbalances between aggressive and passive participants. 
Key Aspects of Volume Bubbles
Color-Coded Action: Red bubbles represent aggressive sell orders (market sellers hitting the bid), and green bubbles represent aggressive buy orders (market buyers lifting the offer).
Size Representation: The size of the bubble corresponds to the volume of the trade, allowing traders to quickly spot large, significant transactions.
Dynamic Customization: Bubbles can be displayed as 2D dots or 3D bubbles, and they can represent total volume (pie chart style showing buy vs. sell split) or net volume delta.
Real-Time Data Flow: They display trades on the best bid/offer (BBO) as they occur, facilitating rapid identification of buying or selling pressure.

Liquidity Heatmap :
The Bookmap Liquidity Heatmap is a visualization tool that maps historical and real-time Level 2 order book data (limit orders) onto a chart using color intensity. It allows traders to see where large buy/sell orders (liquidity) are resting, providing insights into potential support/resistance, market maker activity, and "spoofing" (fake orders). 
Key Features of the Liquidity Heatmap
Visualizing Market Depth (DOM): It displays the full depth of the order book, showing both current limit orders and the history of how those orders were added, moved, or canceled.
Color-Coded Intensity: High liquidity areas are typically shown in bright colors (red/orange), while lower liquidity is represented by colder colors (blue/black). This indicates where large market participants are active.
Historical Tracking: The heatmap records past order book states, allowing traders to analyze how liquidity influenced price action over time.

## What has changed?

### 1. New Volume Intelligence (`VolumeTracker`)
Instead of just showing the immediate resting volume from the current snapshot, we now build a continuous history of every price level. For each price level, we track:
- **`peak_qty`**: The highest order volume ever seen at this level.
- **`added`**: Total volume of newly placed resting orders over time.
- **`filled_cancelled`**: Total volume of orders that have been removed/executed from the book.
- **`current_qty`**: The actual volume remaining right now.

*Impact*: Bubbles scale relative to their `peak_qty`, but drop their opacity/color depth as orders are filled or cancelled. This helps you identify levels that once held massive liquidity barriers even after the orders execute.

### 2. Interactive `BubbleView` & Tooltips
- The heatmap image and standard profile bar were fully removed in favor of `BubbleView`.
- As you hover your mouse inside the plot, a crosshair automatically tracks your cursor.
- When you hover near a bubble, a styled tooltip snaps into view, showing the full breakdown of `VolumeTracker` statistics (Current Qty, Peak, Added, Filled/Cancelled).

### 3. Big Move Alerts
Our `VolumeTracker` now continuously computes an activity score (`added` + `filled_cancelled`) for every price level. 
- It calculates an **adaptive threshold** (`mean + 2σ`) based on the current market's activity levels.
- When the mid-price converges within 2 ticks of any highly active level, the terminal fires a **Big Move Alert**.
- These are displayed both as an orange banner at the top of the interface and as persistent "Star" markers `★` directly on the plot.

### 4. 50-Level Scrollable DOM
The static Order Book was rebuilt with a `QScrollArea`.
- It now manages **50 complete levels** (L1→L50) for both Bid and Ask sides.
- On launch, the widget automatically scrolls to the center to show just the top 10 levels closest to the spread.
